Unit 1: Basic Concept of Teaching

  • Concept and characteristics of teaching
  • Principles and Marks of good teaching
  • Maxim of teaching

  • Meaning and components of model of teaching
  • Basic Model of teaching as Inquiry training model (ITM) and Concept attainment model (CAM)

  • Team teaching‚ÄîConcept and steps of team teaching
  • Advantages of team teaching

  • Meaning and importance
  • Criteria or essential of a good lesson plan
  • Types of lesson plan‚ÄîSkill lesson, Knowledge and appreciation lesson
  • Herbertian approach of lesson plan
  • Preparation of lesson plan of any two subjects of school level
  • New trends in teaching as Mobile learning, Blended learning, Flipped and Google classroom

üéì Learning Outcomes

‚≠ê Credits: 5 (L: 4, T: 1, P: 0)

  • Needs and importance of research report

Measurement and Evaluation in Education

üìñ Nature: Core
‚≠ê Credits: 5 (L: 4, T: 1, P: 0)
üíØ Total Marks: 100 (External: 70, Internal: 30)

üéØ Course Objectives

  • To acquaint with the power of observation and judgment
  • To familiarized with test and norms construction
  • To test the ability and power of interest
  • To know the nature of learner with their attitude and creativity

Unit 1: Measurement and Evaluation

  • Concept, importance and functions of measurement
  • Scale of measurement as Nominal, ordinal, interval and ratio scale
  • Sources of errors in measurement
  • Types of measurement
  • Meaning, importance and functions of Evaluation
  • Principles of evaluation
  • Types of evaluation - Formative and summative evaluation, Norm-referenced and criterion-referenced evaluation
  • Relation and differences between measurement and evaluation

Unit 2: Test Construction and Standardization

  • Test construction and standardization‚Äîmeaning, nature, steps
  • Criteria of a good test
  • Teacher made test vs. standardized test
  • Item analysis and selection of test items
  • Validity and Reliability of test - Types, methods, factors affecting, relationship
  • Meaning and significance of norms
  • Types of norms and their uses

Unit 3: Measurement of Educational Achievement Test

  • Meaning and function of Achievement test
  • Objectives of achievement test (Bloom's taxonomy)
  • Construction of an achievement test
  • Types of educational achievement test
  • Measurement of interest
  • Interest inventory‚ÄîKuder interest inventories

Unit 4: Personality and Intelligence Test/Measurement

  • Concept of personality measurement
  • Types of personality measurement - Subjective, objective, projective techniques, situational
  • Uses of personality test
  • Concept of measurement of intelligence
  • Uses of intelligence measurement
  • Types of intelligence test
  • Binet-Simon test of 1916 and 1960 revision of Stanford-Binet test
  • Wechsler intelligence scale, Age scale and point scales

Unit 5: Aptitude, Attitude and Creativity Test

  • Meaning and nature of Aptitude test
  • Types of aptitude test - General and special aptitude test
  • Uses/educational significance of aptitude test
  • Concept of Attitude test
  • Construction of Attitude scales by Thurston method and Likert Method
  • Measurement of creative thinking
  • Measurement of creative thinking for different levels of students

üéì Learning Outcomes

  • Able to learn calculative life
  • Develop the competitive mind among learner
  • Develop creativity, attitude and aptitude among learner
  • Able to learn the power of skill and approach
